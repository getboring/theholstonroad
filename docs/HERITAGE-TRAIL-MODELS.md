# Heritage Music Trail Models — Research Brief

**Date:** 2026-04-28
**Purpose:** Survey of how heritage music road trails actually operate, then identify the simplest workable model for The Holston Road and propose adjustments to the v2 execution plan.

---

## Why this matters

The v2 execution plan (`EXECUTION-PLAN.md`) defaulted to a Crooked-Road-shaped operating model: anchor venues + audio + interactive map + digital passport + custom-built app. That model is heavy on partnerships, MOUs, and custom dev. Other heritage trails have shipped with much lighter operating models and gotten more reach for less money.

This brief surveys six models and proposes a simplified hybrid.

---

## The Six Models

### 1. Mississippi Blues Trail — the *marker network* model

- **Founded** 2006 by the Mississippi Blues Commission.
- **Scale** 210+ interpretive markers placed at sites of historical significance to blues; 14 markers placed *outside* Mississippi (California to Maine, Norway to France).
- **Operating model:** No venue partnerships required. Each marker is an interpretive panel with researched text + images. Two named scholars (Jim O'Neal and Scott Barretta, former *Living Blues* editors) write all marker text with an editorial team. Marker locations chosen by a panel of blues scholars and historians.
- **Funding:** NEA + NEH + Mississippi DOT + Federal Highway Administration + AT&T + Delta State University + Mississippi Development Authority Tourism + **local community co-sponsorship per marker**.
- **Why it works:** The product *is* the markers. No app. No passport (until recent). No venue dependencies. State + corporate + local money flows because the unit (marker + co-sponsorship) is small, fundable, and maps to civic pride.
- **Constraints:** Requires roadside ROW permission (state DOT cooperation), sustained scholarly editorial capacity, a commission structure to vet locations.

### 2. Tennessee Music Pathways — the *state-curated catalog* model

- **Run by:** Tennessee Department of Tourist Development (TDTD).
- **Scale:** 500+ locations identified, hundreds designated, 7 genres (blues, bluegrass, country, gospel, soul, rockabilly, rock 'n' roll), **all 95 Tennessee counties**.
- **Operating model:** TDTD curates a master catalog. Sites apply or are nominated. State markets the trail collectively under one brand. Partner DMOs cross-promote.
- **Why it matters to us:** **TN already has this.** Our anchor sites likely qualify. Birthplace of Country Music Museum, Bristol, Carter Family Fold (just over the line in VA but networked) are already part of similar designations.
- **Implication:** Holston Road must position *relative to* TN Music Pathways, not parallel. Easiest path: get every Holston Road anchor designated as a Pathways site, then use Pathways' state-tourism distribution as a free megaphone. Cody owns at least one site (Rocky Mount) so this is achievable.

### 3. The Crooked Road — the *anchor-tier* model

- **Scale:** 9 Major Venues + 60+ Affiliated Venues & Festivals + ~25 wayside exhibits + 330-mile route.
- **Operating model:** Three-tier hierarchy. 501(c)(3) nonprofit. Curates programming that already existed (Floyd Country Store Friday Night Jamboree, Galax Old Fiddlers' Convention, Carter Family Fold). One flagship event (Mountains of Music Homecoming, since 2014). Has a navigation app.
- **Funding:** $9.2M total annual economic impact (Va Tech 2016 study) supports state/regional/foundation funding. ~108 FTE jobs. Va Tech impact study is the most-cited asset.
- **Why it works:** The hierarchy gives small venues belonging without diluting brand. State trailblazer signage gives it quasi-official status.
- **Constraints (the ones our v1/v2 plan inherits):** High partnership-MOU overhead. Requires staff. App is custom build. Passport requires infra.

### 4. Americana Music Triangle — the *multi-loop umbrella* model

- **Scale:** ~1,500 miles, 9 genres, **5 distinct driving trails** under one brand (New Orleans–Natchez, Vicksburg–Memphis, Memphis–Nashville, Nashville–Muscle Shoals, Tupelo–New Orleans).
- **Operating model:** "Gold Record Road" master brand. Each loop is an independent self-guided product. Branded by tourism marketers and supported by National Trust for Historic Preservation.
- **Why it's interesting:** **You don't need every site for every loop to ship.** Each loop is a complete product on its own. If 4 sites participate in one loop, that loop ships; the others wait.
- **Implication:** The Holston Road could be three short loops — *The River Loop* (Kingsport area), *The Road Loop* (Bristol–Abingdon corridor), *The March Loop* (Sycamore Shoals → Rocky Mount) — under one master brand. Each ~25 miles, each completable in 2–3 hours, each shippable independently.

### 5. Muscle Shoals & Atchafalaya — the *National Heritage Area* model

- **Federal designation:** Congressionally approved (Muscle Shoals 2009, Atchafalaya 2006). NPS-recognized.
- **Operating model:** University-housed administration. Muscle Shoals NHA is housed at University of North Alabama (UNA serves as fiduciary). Atchafalaya covers 15 parishes. Both run grant programs for local preservation projects, develop walking tours / driving tours / brochures, host the **Roots of American Music Trail** (a self-guided cell-phone audio tour, no app required).
- **Why it matters:** This is the long-game endgame. **Northeast Tennessee does not currently have a National Heritage Area.** The 250th + Overmountain Men + Watauga Association + first state west of the mountains story is a *legitimate* NHA designation candidate. ETSU could host the way UNA hosts MSNHA. This is a 5–10 year political track but the application can begin now and pairs with AR250 momentum.
- **Audio model worth stealing immediately:** Roots of American Music Trail uses a self-guided cell-phone audio tour — no app, no passport, just call-a-number / scan-a-QR / open-a-URL audio at each site. Dramatically simpler than what our plan currently spec's.

### 6. Atchafalaya Water Heritage Trail — the *50-stop self-guided driving trail* (lite version of the NHA model)

- **50+ stops** along a self-guided driving trail. No app. No passport. Just a mapped driving route with waysides and partner sites.
- **Why it's interesting:** Proof you can run a real heritage trail with zero custom software. A printed map and a website with site pages is enough. The water trail uses paddling access points + interpretive markers + partner restaurants/music venues all listed plainly on one site.

## Common patterns across all six models

1. **Markers + waysides do most of the cultural work.** Apps and passports are nice-to-have, not must-have. None of these trails *started* with an app.
2. **Curate, don't create.** Every successful trail networks events and venues that already exist. The Crooked Road didn't invent the Floyd Jamboree; the Blues Trail didn't invent Dockery Plantation.
3. **One named scholar/historian fronts the editorial.** Mississippi Blues Trail → Jim O'Neal + Scott Barretta. Va Crooked Road → Joe Wilson. This is the credibility unit, and it's small and cheap.
4. **State DOT cooperation is the unsung superpower.** Trailblazer signs and roadside marker permissions convert marketing into infrastructure.
5. **University fiscal sponsor model is durable.** UNA hosts Muscle Shoals NHA. ETSU could host Holston Road equivalent.
6. **Per-marker / per-site local co-sponsorship splits the funding burden** beautifully. A $5,000 marker becomes $1,500 NEA + $1,500 state + $2,000 local civic group / county tourism. Repeats forever.
7. **Audio is delivered via QR/URL, not app.** Apps are death; cell-phone web audio works fine.

## What we should adapt and how

### Recommended simplification: a hybrid Mississippi-Blues-Trail × Americana-Triangle model

**Drop from current v2 plan:**
- Custom-built digital passport infra. Use a SaaS (LoyalBrew, Local Explorers, or Driftscape) when ready, or ship paper-only indefinitely. **Saves ~$8–15k in dev.**
- Custom interactive map. Use embedded Google My Maps for v1; upgrade to MapLibre only if traffic justifies. **Saves ~3 weeks of dev.**
- Custom audio player. Use a `<audio>` tag with QR codes pointing at hosted MP3s. **Saves another week.**
- Single-trail framing. Replace with **3 named loops under one brand**.
- Custom user accounts and check-in DB until Phase 6.

**Add from these models:**
- **A marker network** as the central physical product. Aim for 12 markers in Year 1 (one per major site + one per chapter wayside theme), 25 by Year 3, 50 by Year 5. Each marker = $3,000–6,000, funded NEA + state + local civic co-sponsor.
- **One named historian** as editorial face. Dr. Carroll Van West or designate. Their name on the markers, the books, the website. This is the Joe Wilson role.
- **Three short loops** instead of one 75-mile trail:
  - *The River* (Kingsport: Netherland Inn → Long Island → Exchange Place) ~15 mi
  - *The Agreement* (Elizabethton: Sycamore Shoals → Tipton-Haynes → Carter Mansion) ~20 mi
  - *The March* (Bristol → Rocky Mount → Sycamore Shoals along Overmountain route) ~35 mi
  - Each loop ships independently. The 75-mile master loop combines all three.
- **TN Music Pathways alignment** — get every anchor designated as a TN Music Pathways site. This is *free distribution* through state tourism marketing.
- **National Heritage Area research track** as a Phase 6+ endgame. Open conversations with NPS, ETSU, and TN delegation in 2027.
- **Cell-phone audio** at every marker via QR → R2-hosted MP3 + transcript page. No player UI needed.

## Proposed v3 plan adjustments

The v2 plan is good. These are deltas, not a rewrite.

| Phase | v2 spec | v3 adjustment |
|---|---|---|
| **Phase 0** | Domain, sponsor, EBC, advisory board, narratives drafted | **+ Apply for TN Music Pathways designation for Rocky Mount, Sycamore Shoals, Netherland Inn, Exchange Place. Free distribution starts Day 1.** |
| **Phase 1** | Brand reset, 8 waysides on site, TDOT signage application | **Reframe: 8 *digital* waysides on site → *physical* marker pilot for 3 waysides at Rocky Mount as proof-of-concept. NEA Our Town grant application begins.** |
| **Phase 2** | MapLibre + custom audio + itinerary | **Google My Maps embed instead of MapLibre. QR-to-MP3 instead of audio player component. 3 loops, not 1 master loop. Saves ~4 weeks.** |
| **Phase 3** | 12 audio stories + print map + 4 anchor MOUs + AR250 | **Same, plus: marker design system finalized; first 3 marker locations selected with TDOT input; National Trust for Historic Preservation outreach (they backed Americana Music Triangle).** |
| **Phase 4** | Soft launch at Sept 25 Muster | **Same, plus: marker dedications at the 3 pilot sites coincide with launch — *physical* news, not just digital news. Local press loves a ribbon cutting.** |
| **Phase 5** | Stripe memberships + paper passport + podcast | **Same, but: digital passport via SaaS (LoyalBrew or similar, ~$100/mo) when paper-passport demand exceeds 200/mo. Don't build, buy.** |
| **Phase 6** | AR250 amplification | **Add: National Heritage Area feasibility study. Engage TN federal delegation. Aim for designation by 2030 (Battle of Kings Mountain 250th).** |

### Budget impact

Roughly $8–12k in custom dev avoided across Year 1. Reallocate:
- $4k to first 1–2 physical markers (the most powerful single asset of the entire program; produces a tangible, photographable, press-eligible artifact)
- $2k to marker design system (template + sign vendor + DOT approval)
- $2k to NHA feasibility outreach + university hosting conversation

### Operating-model impact

**Reduces partner-MOU pressure dramatically.** Markers can live on state/county/city ROW with public-agency permits — they don't require venue partnerships. So if BCMM declines to be Chapter 2, we put a marker on State Street ROW that tells the 1927 Sessions story regardless. This protects the brand from any single partner's veto.

## What we still need to verify

- TDOT marker program: confirm process, timeline, costs, design constraints. (Compare to MS DOT's Blues Trail collaboration.)
- TN Music Pathways application process and lead times.
- Whether any Holston-area site is *already* on TN Music Pathways. (Likely yes; check before applying.)
- ETSU's appetite to host a future NHA fiduciary-style relationship. (Talk to ETSU's Center for Appalachian Studies & Services.)
- National Trust for Historic Preservation regional contact (they backed Americana Music Triangle).
- Whether Carter Family Fold's adjacency to the Tri-Cities lets us cross-list with the Crooked Road in a non-competitive way (e.g., "where the trails meet" framing at Bristol).
